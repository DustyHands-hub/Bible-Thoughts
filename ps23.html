Home Psalm 23

Passage

1 The Lord is my shepherd, I lack nothing.
2 He takes me to lush pastures, he leads me to refreshing water.
3 He restores my strength. He leads me down the right paths for the sake of his reputation.
4 Even when I must walk through the darkest valley, I fear no danger, for you are with me; your rod and your staff reassure me.

Notes

V.1
David is the Psalmist here, and he grew up as a shepherd boy. So the allusion of a sheep-shepherd relationship was one very familiar to him.
The verb translated as "want," eh-sar, comes from the word khaser meaning to lack/need something. See Deut. 8:9; Prov. 10:21.
Question: What does a shepherd do? What does it mean for the Lord to be our Shepherd?

V.2
A shepherd will take care of his sheep. He leads them to pastures to eat, to water to drink, he protects the herd.
The word "still" here is menuhowt in Hebrew. It refers to a place of rest. See 1 Kings 8:56; 1 Chronicles 22:9.
Question: In what way does God provide for us? Particularly rest?

V.3
The word here, strength, is naphshi. It comes from nephesh, which means throat, but often refers to the soul or entire person. And restore has a sense of returning to a state you were once in. But it isn't for our sake that He restores our souls, it's for the sake of His name. Everyone in creation is for His glory.
Question: How are our souls restored? What state are they in now, and what state are they being restored to? How does doing this glorify God?

V.4
This verse pictures the shepherd taking his sheep through a dark ravine where predators might lurk.
There is debate about what the Hebrew here means. Valley of darkness? Shadow of death? The word is used in some places to refer to a deep darkness, and others to refer to the land of the dead. See Job 3:5; 10:21-22.
Question: What sort of "valley" would David be walking through at this time? Do we walk through valleys similar to this today?
